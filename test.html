About the University of Oklahoma

The University of Oklahoma (OU) is a public research university in Norman, Oklahoma. Founded in 1890, it had existed in Oklahoma Territory near Indian Territory for 17 years before the two became the state of Oklahoma. In Fall 2018, the university had 31,702 students enrolled, most at its main campus in Norman.

Employing nearly 3,000 faculty members, the school offers 152 baccalaureate programs, 160 master's programs, 75 doctorate programs, and 20 majors at the first professional level. David Boren, a former U.S. Senator and Oklahoma Governor, served as the university's president from 1994 to 2018. James L. Gallogly succeeded Boren on July 1, 2018, only to retire in May 2019. Joseph Harroz Jr. was appointed as interim president in May 2019 and named the university's 15th president in May 2020.

History

The University of Oklahoma was established in Norman in 1890 by the Oklahoma Territorial Legislature. The university was initially established as a university for the education of white students. The university officially opened its doors on September 15, 1890, with four faculty members offering instruction to 100 students.

The university's first president, David Ross Boyd, arrived in Norman on August 6, 1892, and the first building, University Hall, was completed in 1893. The university established a School of Pharmacy in 1893, a School of Engineering in 1909, and a School of Medicine in 1910.

Academics

The University of Oklahoma is a large residential, research university. The university consists of fifteen colleges, including 152 majors such as meteorology, geology, petroleum engineering, energy management, architecture, medicine, law, business, and many more. The university has a large student life program, with over 350 student organizations. OU is home to a wide variety of museums, including the Fred Jones Jr. Museum of Art, Sam Noble Oklahoma Museum of Natural History, and the National Weather Center.

In addition to 152 majors to choose from, the University of Oklahoma also has a nationally recognized Honors College featuring its own dedicated faculty, dormitories, and writing center. Every student from any major can apply to the college; if accepted the student is eligible to take honors classes and graduate cum laude. In order to graduate with honors, the student must complete 18 credit hours of honors classes and submit an honors thesis. OU also has a student-run newspaper, The Oklahoma Daily; and a student-run radio station, KGOU.

Athletics

The University of Oklahoma has a strong tradition in athletics, with the university's sports teams being known as the Oklahoma Sooners. The Sooners have won 7 NCAA Division I Football Championships and have produced 7 Heisman Trophy winners, including Sam Bradford, Jason White, and Baker Mayfield.

The men's gymnastics team has won 12 national championships, more than any other program, and the softball team has won 6 national championships, including back-to-back titles in 2016 and 2017. The Sooners have also won national championships in baseball, men's golf, and wrestling.
